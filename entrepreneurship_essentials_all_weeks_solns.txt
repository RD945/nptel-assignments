**QUESTION 4**  
Identify the correct statement from the following: What is meant by the construct limited liability in the context of a limited liability form of legal entity?

a. The liability of the company is limited to its resources.  
b. The liability of any owner is limited to his/her share of the business at any point in time.  
c. The liability of any owner is limited to his/her proportional shareholding in the company.  
d. The liability of a company is limited to the market value of its assets.  

**Correct Answer:** b. The liability of any owner is limited to his/her share of the business at any point in time.  

**Detailed Solution:**  
The liability of any owner is limited to his/her share of the business at any point in time. His/her personal assets are not at risk for the recovery of any company debts.  
Imagine a person, X, who has invested Rs. 1.00 lakh in a business and received 10,000 shares assuming a face value or par value of Rs. 10 for each share, constituting 20% of the shareholding. Suppose the company borrows Rs. 100 lakh from a bank and defaults. Let us assume the bank obtains a court decree to recover the money from the company. The court is entitled to receive proceeds by selling the company’s assets but cannot touch the personal assets of X or any other shareholders. Consequently, in the liquidation process, X may have to forfeit their shares without affecting personal assets.  
Option “c” in the question suggests that liability is proportional to shareholding, implying that X must pay Rs. 10 lakh to the bank. However, option “b” accurately states that liability is limited to the shares one owns and not the shareholding percentage. Of course, the value of the shares may change over time.

**QUESTION 10**  
Which of the following can create the least of Entry Barriers?

a. Brand loyalty  
b. Business process management at the shop floor  
c. Ownership of intellectual property  
d. Logistic advantage  

**Correct Answer:** b. Business process management at the shop floor  

**Detailed Solution:**  
Business process management (BPM) is primarily a tool for optimizing and streamlining internal processes within a company. While BPM can enhance efficiency and agility, it may not necessarily create significant entry barriers. The transparency and standardization promoted by BPM could potentially lower barriers, as it facilitates easier understanding and replication of processes by new entrants. Entry barriers typically involve factors like economies of scale, proprietary technologies, or strong brand presence, which are beyond the scope of what BPM primarily addresses.

**QUESTION 4**  
Select the INCORRECT statement.

a. Markets with perfect competition have homogenous products without any pricing power.  
b. Oligopolistic markets have differentiated product types with high barrier for new entrants and limited pricing power.  
c. Monopolists have unique and simple type of products with great barrier for new entrants.  
d. Companies in a perfect competition market have high pricing power and high entry barriers against competition.  

**Correct Answer:** d. Companies in a perfect competition market have high pricing power and high entry barriers against competition.  

**Detailed Solution:**  
Perfect competition market has many sellers and many buyers. If one of the buyers increases price, customers will go to another seller. They do not have pricing power and do not enjoy entry barrier. A new venture can easily enter into that market. Oligopolistic market has limited number of sellers with product differentiations. Because of the differentiations, they enjoy some pricing power with limited chance of losing customers for price increase. New entrant find it very difficult to enter into an oligopolistic market. There can be no doubt that monopolists have unique and simple product with no competition.

**QUESTION 8**  
A company has an authorized capital of Rupees 10,000. A, B, and C are the three cofounder shareholders. What are their percentage holdings in the company?

c. 26.67%, 6.67%, and 26.67% respectively

C holds 20/50 × 100 = 40%

**QUESTION 9**  
The company, Your Company, has an authorized capital of Rupees 1000. A, B, and C are the three cofounder shareholders. A holds 20 shares of Rupees 10 each, B holds 10 shares of Rupees 10 each, and C holds 20 shares of Rupees 10 each. There is no other shareholder in the company. Your Company has negotiated an investment round with an angel. The angel has valued Your Company at Rupees 10,000 before investment. They agreed to invest Rupees 10,000 for 50% of the company. How Your Company will issue shares to the angel to avail of this investment?

a. All cofounders give away 50% of their shares to the angel. Therefore, the cofounders together hold 50%, and the angel holds 50% after the allotment of shares.  
b. Your company creates 50 new shares of Rupees 10 and allot to the angel.  
c. Your company creates 500 new shares of Rupees 10 and allot to the angel.  
d. Your company creates 100 new shares of Rupees 100 and allot to the angel.  

**Correct Answer:** b. Your company creates 50 new shares of Rupees 10 and allot to the angel.  

**Detailed Solution:**  
Post investment, the angel should hold 50% of equity capital and the three cofounders together should hold 50%. Therefore, total number of shares after allotment = (20 + 10 + 20) × 2 = 100 shares. Existing founders hold 50 shares, so the angel should get 50 new shares of Rupees 10 each.

**QUESTION 10**  
An angel agrees to invest Rupees 40 million in a startup at a pre-money valuation of Rupees 120 million. All the cofounders together hold 750 shares of Rupees 10 each. This is the first-time investment for the company and there is no other existing shareholder at present. How many shares are to be allotted to the angel for this round of investment?

a. 250  
b. 360  
c. 200  
d. 350  

**Correct Answer:** a. 250  

**Detailed Solution:**  
Post-money valuation = 120 + 40 = 160 million.  
Angel should receive 40/160 = 25% of post-money equity.  
Founders’ 750 shares represent 75% of post-money equity.  
Total post-money shares = 750 / 0.75 = 1000 shares.  
Shares to angel = 1000 – 750 = 250.
