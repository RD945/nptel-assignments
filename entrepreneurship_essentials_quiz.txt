b) The liability of any owner is limited to his/her share of the business at any point in
time.
c) The liability of any owner is limited to his/her proportional shareholding in the

d) The liability of a company is limited to the market value of its assets.
Correct Answer: b
Detailed Solution:
NPTEL Online Certification Courses
Indian Institute of Technology Kharagpur
The liability of any owner is limited to his/her share of the business at any point in time. His/her/ their personal
assets are not at risk for the recovery of any company debts.
Imagine a person, 'X,' who has invested Rs. 1.00 lakh in a business and received 10,000 shares (assuming a
face value or par value of Rs. 10/- for each share), constituting 20% of the shareholding. Suppose the company
borrows Rs. 100 lakh from a bank and defaults. Let's assume the bank obtains a court decree to recover the
money from the company. The court is entitled to receive proceeds by selling the company's assets but cannot
touch the personal assets of 'X' or any other shareholders. Consequently, in the liquidation process, 'X' may
have to forfeit their shares without affecting personal assets.

QUESTION 4:
Select the INCORRECT statement.
a) Markets with perfect competition have homogenous products without any pricing power.
b) Oligopolistic markets have differentiated product types with high barrier for new entrants and
limited pricing power.
c) Monopolists have unique and simple type of products with great barrier for new entrants.
d) Companies in a perfect competition market have high pricing power and high entry barriers
against competition.
Answer: d
Detailed Solution
Perfect competition market has many sellers and many buyers. If one of the buyers increases
price, customers will go to another seller. They do not have pricing power and do not enjoy entry
barrier. A new venture can easily enter into that market. Oligopolistic market has limited number
of sellers with product differentiations. Because of the differentiations, they enjoy some pricing
power with limited chance of losing customers for price increase. New entrant find it very
difficult enter into an oligopolistic market. There can be no doubt that monopolists have unique
and simple product with no competition.
